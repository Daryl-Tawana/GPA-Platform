Documentation & Methodology

Formulas, variable definitions, assumptions, and limitations

Purpose of the Dashboard

ZARIP (Zimbabwe Agricultural Retained Income Platform) is a static, client-side analytical dashboard designed to model and visualise the net income retained by smallholder tobacco growers in Zimbabwe after accounting for all production costs, marketing deductions, and contract loan recoveries.

The central research question is: "How much income does the tobacco grower actually retain after all costs and deductions?" The dashboard addresses a significant transparency gap in smallholder tobacco value chains, where growers may not have clear visibility into what they actually receive after the contractor, auction floor, and government levy claims are settled.

Methodology

The model uses a farm income and gross margin approach. The analytical flow is:

  1. Calculate gross revenue from all tobacco sales channels.
  2. Subtract all variable production costs (cash-based) to arrive at gross margin.
  3. Subtract contract loan recovery to arrive at net retained income.
  4. Compare against break-even thresholds to assess viability.

The model is cash-cost based. Family labour and own-supply fuelwood are treated as cash costs where values are provided. Non-cash opportunity costs (e.g. land rent foregone) are not modelled.

Core Formulas

Gross Revenue
Gross Revenue = (Contract kg × Contract Price/kg) + (Auction kg × Auction Price/kg)
Total Production Cost
Total Production Cost = Seed + Fertiliser + Chemicals + Labour + Fuelwood/Curing + Transport + Packaging + Auction Charges + Levies
Contract Recovery
Contract Recovery = Amount deducted by contractor (= Input Loan Advanced, unless partial)
Net Retained Income
Net Retained Income = Gross Revenue − Total Production Cost − Contract Recovery
Profit Margin (%)
Profit Margin = (Net Retained Income / Gross Revenue) × 100
Cost per kg
Cost per kg = Total Production Cost / Total kg Harvested
Revenue per kg
Revenue per kg = Gross Revenue / Total kg Harvested
Break-even Price per kg
Break-even Price/kg = (Total Production Cost + Contract Recovery) / Total kg Harvested
Break-even Yield per Hectare
Break-even Yield/ha = (Total Production Cost + Contract Recovery) / (Avg Selling Price × Hectares Planted)
Loan Recovery Ratio (%)
Loan Recovery Ratio = (Contract Recovery / Input Loan Advanced) × 100
Channel Revenue Share (%)
Channel Share = (Channel Revenue / Total Revenue) × 100

Assumptions

  • All prices are expressed in USD. No currency conversion or inflation adjustment is applied.
  • Family labour is treated as a cash cost if a value is provided; otherwise it is excluded (understating true economic cost).
  • Fuelwood is treated as a cash cost. Growers who self-supply wood from their own land may face a lower actual cash outlay, in which case the model will overstate costs slightly.
  • Contract recovery is assumed to equal the full input loan advanced unless a partial recovery is specified.
  • The model does not account for multi-season debt rollover or interest accrual on input loans.
  • Auction charges and levies are as reported by the grower; these can vary by floor and registration status.
  • Yield figures are post-curing (cured leaf weight), not green weight.
  • The quality grade score is an indicative index and does not directly adjust prices in the base model (it is shown for contextual interpretation only).

Limitations

  • The model is static and illustrative. It is built from a small set of anonymised scenarios and does not represent a statistically representative sample of Zimbabwe's smallholder tobacco sector.
  • It does not model non-cash opportunity costs such as land rent, own capital, or time value of money.
  • Prices are single-season point estimates. Real auction prices vary by grade, date, and floor — averaging masks this variation.
  • The dashboard cannot account for side-marketing (where growers sell outside the registered contract), which affects declared revenues and recovery calculations.
  • Contract terms vary considerably by contractor. The model uses a simplified full-recovery assumption that may not reflect partial or renegotiated recoveries.
  • The model does not incorporate macroeconomic risks such as exchange rate depreciation, input price inflation, or climate shocks beyond the yield sensitivity slider.

Technical Architecture

  • index.html — All page structure and section markup
  • style.css — Responsive layout, typography, and visual theme
  • app.js — All calculation logic, DOM rendering, and event handling
  • data/grower-data.json — Static data file with grower records and scenario definitions

The dashboard is fully static and requires no backend, database, or authentication. All calculations occur in the browser. It is deployable to GitHub Pages or any static hosting service by committing the files to a repository.
